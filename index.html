What Nexus gives you

Three things. That's it.

01
💬 Talk

Real conversations on topics that actually matter.

Not small talk. Nexus is built around focused interest domains — Social Discussion, Environment, Startups, Art, and more. Inside each, groups go deep on one specific topic. No marks. No syllabus.

Just honest discussion between people who chose to be there.

What it looks like
💬 # group-poverty-inequality
@Alex
The Grattan report on intergenerational poverty is wild — did anyone read it?
@Jordan
Yeah the housing cost section. I wrote notes — want to share here?
@Sam
Please do. Also thinking about structural vs personal agency from last week...
02
🤝 Connect

Find your people. Across schools, year levels, regions.

Nexus is independent of any school. You'll meet students from across Australia who share your interests — not just whoever sits next to you in class.

Groups are kept small and tight-knit on purpose. Every member chose to be there for the same reason you did.

03
🚀 Build

Take it offline. Connect with the world.

Groups meet in real life — cafés, parks, libraries, wherever. And through the admin team, Nexus connects students to external organisations and other school communities.

The conversations you start here can go further than you'd expect.

Where it can go
☕ Casual meetups — café, beach, park. No permission needed.
🏛️ Community events — debates, reading nights, pitch sessions.
🌐 External connections — NGOs, organisations, other schools.
🤝 Cross-school collaboration — meet people across Australia.
